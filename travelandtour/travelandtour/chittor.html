Famous Places to Visit in Chittorgarh

  1. Chittorgarh Fort
    One of the largest forts in India and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Chittorgarh Fort is steeped in history and known for its impressive architecture, magnificent palaces, and legendary tales of valor and sacrifice.

  2. Vijay Stambh (Tower of Victory)
    Erected by Maharana Kumbha to commemorate his victory over the Sultan of Malwa, this imposing tower stands tall within the fort complex, adorned with intricate carvings and offering panoramic views of the surrounding area.

  3. Kirti Stambh (Tower of Fame)
    A Jain tower built in the 12th century, Kirti Stambh is renowned for its elaborate Jain sculptures and inscriptions, showcasing the rich cultural heritage of the region.

  4. Rana Kumbha Palace
    The erstwhile residence of the rulers of Mewar, this grand palace within the fort complex features impressive architecture, intricate designs, and historical significance, including the birthplace of Maharana Udai Singh II, the founder of Udaipur.

  5. Padmini Palace
    Named after Queen Padmini, this palace is famed for its role in Rajput history and the legendary tale of the queen's beauty, which led to the siege of Chittorgarh by Alauddin Khilji. The palace offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and the nearby water reservoirs.
