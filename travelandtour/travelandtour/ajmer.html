Famous Places to Visit in Ajmer

  1. Ajmer Sharif Dargah
    A revered Sufi shrine dedicated to Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti, a 13th-century Sufi saint. It attracts millions of pilgrims from diverse faiths who come to seek blessings and offer prayers at this sacred site.

  2. Ana Sagar Lake
    A picturesque artificial lake commissioned by Anaji Chauhan, the grandfather of Prithviraj Chauhan. Visitors can enjoy boating, serene walks along the lake's promenade, and breathtaking views of the surrounding hills.

  3. Adhai Din Ka Jhonpra
    Originally a Sanskrit college, this monument was converted into a mosque by Sultan Ghori in just two-and-a-half days, giving it its name, "Adhai Din Ka Jhonpra." It's renowned for its Indo-Islamic architecture and intricate carvings.

  4. Taragarh Fort
    Perched atop a steep hill, Taragarh Fort offers panoramic views of Ajmer and its surroundings. It's famous for its massive fortifications, ancient cannons, and the Dargah of Miran Saheb, a revered saint within the fort complex.

  5. Akbari Fort and Museum
    Constructed by Emperor Akbar, this fort now houses a museum showcasing a diverse collection of artifacts, including Mughal and Rajput weaponry, sculptures, paintings, and historical manuscripts, providing insights into Ajmer's rich cultural heritage.
